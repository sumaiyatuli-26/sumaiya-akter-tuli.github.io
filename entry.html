Abstract

Entry into the Martian atmosphere at hypersonic velocities generates significant aerothermodynamic loads, specifically at the vehicle's stagnation point. This research develops a computational framework for estimating radiative and convective heat flux using the Sutton-Grave correlations. We analyze the impact of CO2 dissociation on the shock-layer temperature and evaluate the effectiveness of ablation-based thermal protection systems (TPS) during the peak heating phase of the trajectory.

I. Theoretical Heat Transfer Framework

The convective heat flux $\dot{q}$ during hypersonic flight is a function of the atmospheric density $\rho$ and the cube of the free-stream velocity $V_{\infty}$. The relationship is governed by the blunt-body aerodynamic theory:

\[ \dot{q}_{total} \approx K \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{\infty}}{R_n}} \cdot V_{\infty}^3 + \epsilon \sigma (T_{shock}^4 - T_{wall}^4) \]

The Stagnation Heat Flux Equation for Equilibrium and Radiative Flow.

II. Computational Entry Solver

This Python implementation provides a numerical solution for the heat load profile, simulating a vehicle entering the Martian atmosphere at Mach 25. 

import math

def sutton_grave_solver(rho, v, Rn):
    # K: Martian Atmospheric Constant (1.89e-4)
    K = 1.892e-4 
    
    # Calculating Convective Heat Flux (W/cm^2)
    q_conv = K * math.sqrt(rho / Rn) * (v**3)
    return q_conv

# Simulation Parameters: Altitude 35km
rho_mars = 0.00021 # Atmospheric density (kg/m^3)
v_entry = 5800     # Velocity (m/s)
nose_radius = 1.2  # Blunt body radius (m)

flux = sutton_grave_solver(rho_mars, v_entry, nose_radius)
print(f"Stagnation Heat Flux Estimate: {flux:.4f} W/cm^2")

III. Critical Barriers in Atmospheric Entry

01 // Ionized Plasma Blackout

The shock layer generates a plasma sheath that reflects electromagnetic waves. Maintaining telemetry during this phase requires Magnetic Field Windowing to bypass the ionization frequency.

02 // Non-Equilibrium Chemical Dissociation

CO2 molecules break into CO and O within the shock layer. This endothermic reaction creates a Chemical Non-Equilibrium state that significantly complicates heat flux predictions.

03 // Surface Ablation Dynamics

Modeling the mass-loss rate of the heat shield (PICA) requires solving the Stefan Problem, where the boundary between the solid shield and the fluid plasma is constantly moving.

04 // Wake Turbulence Stability

Low-density flow behind the heat shield creates Vortex Shedding. This can cause dynamic instability (wobbling) during the transonic transition, necessitating high-speed RCS correction.

05 // Radiative Heating Dominance

At velocities exceeding 6 km/s, radiative heat transfer from the shock layer becomes larger than convective heating, requiring Spectral Opacity Modeling for accurate shield sizing.

I. Mathematical Derivation

The viability of a traversable wormhole hinges on the violation of the Null Energy Condition (NEC). At the throat radius ($r = b_0$), the Einstein Field Equations yield the following relationship between radial tension ($\tau$) and energy density ($\rho$):

\[ \tau(b_0) - \rho(b_0)c^2 = \frac{c^4}{8\pi G} \frac{1 - b'(b_0)}{b_0^2} \]

For a traversable geometry, we must ensure $b'(b_0) < 1$. This derivation proves that the throat requires an exotic matter component where radial tension exceeds the energy density, effectively counteracting gravitational collapse.

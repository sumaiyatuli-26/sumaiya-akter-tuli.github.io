I. Theoretical Architecture

In standard General Relativity, an Einstein-Rosen bridge is inherently unstable. To maintain a traversable throat radius $b_0$, we define the spacetime geometry using the Morris-Thorne Metric:

\[ ds^2 = -e^{2\Phi(r)} c^2 dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{b(r)}{r}\right)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 (d\theta^2 + \sin^2\theta d\phi^2) \]

Where $\Phi(r)$ is the gravitational redshift function and $b(r)$ is the shape function. For a stable transit, we must ensure that the Null Energy Condition (NEC) is violated locally:

\[ T_{\mu\nu} k^\mu k^\nu < 0 \]

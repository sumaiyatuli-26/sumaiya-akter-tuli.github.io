Research Abstract

This project investigates the stability of traversable Einstein-Rosen bridges within the framework of Lorentzian Topology. By integrating the Morris-Thorne Metric with semi-classical quantum stress tensors, we propose a localized mechanism for sustaining throat geometry against gravitational collapse. The solver simulates negative energy density regimes generated by vacuum polarization (Casimir flux), ensuring tidal forces remain within human biological thresholds for hypothetical interstellar transit.

I. Mathematical Derivation

The fundamental requirement for wormhole traversability is the violation of the Null Energy Condition (NEC). We define the stress-energy tensor $T_{\mu\nu}$ such that it compensates for the Schwarzschild-induced radial inward pressure. The stability condition at the throat radius $r = b_0$ is derived as:

\[ \tau(b_0) - \rho(b_0)c^2 = \frac{c^4}{8\pi G} \frac{1 - b'(b_0)}{b_0^2} \]

For a stable, open throat, the flare-out condition $b'(b_0) < 1$ must be satisfied. This ensures that the geometry opens up away from the throat, preventing the formation of an event horizon.
